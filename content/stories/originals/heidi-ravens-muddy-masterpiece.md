# Heidi and the Ravens’ Muddy Masterpiece

Heidi was an old dog with soft gray ears and careful feet. She didn’t race
squirrels anymore. She didn’t leap over logs, either. Slow walks, warm grass,
and giant naps were more her style. Sometimes she slept so deeply that
butterflies landed right on her nose.

One sunny morning, Heidi found two ravens bouncing around her favourite grassy
hill. One was shiny and neat. Her name was Pepper. The other had one feather
that always stuck straight up. His name was Pickle.

They were pulling long pieces of grass from the ground.

“Are you building a nest?” Heidi asked.

“No,” said Pepper.

“Are you making a hat?”

“We tried that yesterday,” said Pickle. “It looked like a salad on my head.”

The ravens were making a grass rope, but every time they lifted it, the rope fell
apart. Pepper pointed toward the muddy pond. A bright blue spoon sat on a little
island in the middle.

“That’s the finest spoon we’ve ever seen,” Pepper said.

“It shines,” Pickle added. “It scoops. It’s extremely spoonish.”

Their collection already had a bottle cap, a marble, a silver button, and a tiny
bell with no ring left in it. They wanted the spoon too.

“Why not fly to the island?” Heidi asked.

“Yep, we could,” said Pepper.

“But then the story would be super short,” said Pickle.

Heidi liked long, slow stories, so she agreed to help. The ravens tied grass
ropes around sticks and threw them toward the spoon. One went plop. One went
splat. Heidi tossed one with her mouth. It flew three feet, turned around, and
landed behind her.

“Nice backward throw,” Pepper said.

Heidi sat in the warm grass to think. Pickle perched on her back. Pepper perched
on Pickle. They looked like a small feathery tower.

Then Heidi spotted tall grass bending across the water toward the island. “We
don’t need one rope,” she said. “We need a path.”

Pepper and Pickle wove the tall grass together where it grew. Heidi pressed the
ends down with her paws. Slowly they made a narrow green bridge. Pepper hopped
across first. Pickle bounced behind. The bridge dipped, but it held.

They made it to the spoon. Then both ravens grabbed it at once.

“Your end is too high!” said Pepper.

“Your end is too spoony!” said Pickle.

The spoon flipped into the air, flashed in the sunlight, and landed in the mud
beside Heidi. Plop! A brown splash covered Heidi from nose to tail.

The ravens froze. Heidi blinked. A lump of mud slid slowly off one ear. Then she
sneezed—A-CHOO! Three perfect muddy circles appeared on the grass.

Pickle tilted his head. “Art.”

Pepper dipped one foot in mud and stamped a raven print beside the circles.
Pickle added two wing marks. Heidi pressed one paw down, then another. Soon the
grass held a whole muddy picture: a round sun, a crooked tree, two ravens, and
one old dog with very large ears.

They totally forgot about the spoon. Then a tiny warm rain began. The muddy
picture got soft and slipped back into the earth. Clean drops sparkled on the
grass.

Pepper picked up the blue spoon. “Our treasure!”

Pickle looked at the fading picture. “I liked the other treasure too.”

Heidi flopped into the wet grass. “Some treasures you keep. Some you make. And
some you remember during a really good nap.”

The ravens placed the spoon beside Heidi like a royal prize. Pepper curled near
one gray ear, and Pickle curled near the other. Soon all three were asleep beside
the muddy pond, dreaming of grass bridges, backward sticks, and the world’s most
spoonish spoon.
