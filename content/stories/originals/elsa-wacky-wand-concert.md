# Elsa’s Wacky Wand Concert

*An unofficial, playful fan-story featuring Elsa, the Disney princess.*

One sunny afternoon, a snowflake-shaped door appeared in Otherworld. It spun
once, chimed twice, and opened with a soft whoosh. Elsa stepped through in a
sparkling blue dress.

Behind her, the door snapped shut and became a surprised-looking snowman-shaped
shrub. “Well,” Elsa said, “this definitely isn’t Arendelle.”

“You’re in Otherworld!” called Neko, rolling forward. “We’ve got singing
teacups, upside-down rain, and a mayor who’s sometimes a sandwich.”

A bunch of children hurried over. They knew Elsa right away and cheered.

“Will you make a snowstorm?” asked Magic Girl.

“A tiny one,” Elsa said. “It’s way too nice out for frozen socks.”

She lifted one hand. Cool snowflakes floated over the square, but they didn’t
make anybody cold or wet. Each flake landed with a musical note. Plink! Plonk!
Prrring! Soon the square sounded like a gentle bell orchestra.

“We were just about to have a concert,” Neko said. “Our singer’s a teapot, but
she lost her lid and feels shy.”

“I’d love to sing,” said Elsa.

Neko parked herself in the perfect spot. Ta-da—instant stage! The children
flopped onto bright cushions while moon mice, cloud dragons, and the Snail Band
gathered nearby.

Elsa sang a brand-new song about brave little lights, kind friends, and finding
a laugh on a wobbly day. Her voice skipped over the rooftops. Magical
snowflakes danced with every note. They made pictures of kittens, crowns, and
enormous chickens wearing boots.

The children sang the final part with her:

“Shine a little, laugh a lot. Share the magic that you’ve got!”

When the song ended, everybody clapped and whooped. Even the mayor, who was a
cheese sandwich today, clapped his crusts together.

“I brought something for everyone,” Elsa said.

She opened a silver case filled with magic wands. Some were blue and glittery.
Some were green with curly handles. One looked exactly like a wooden spoon.

“Every wand has three rules,” Elsa said. “Be kind with magic. Ask before you use
it on somebody else. And never point it at anybody’s face.”

Each child chose a wand. Magic Girl waved hers, and her shoes began to quack like
ducks. A boy named Theo made bubbles that smelled like cinnamon toast. Another
child gave three cabbages tiny bow ties. The cabbages looked extremely proud.

Then the wooden-spoon wand went, “Ah... ah... ACHOO!”

Purple stars bounced across the square. Wherever one landed, something became
delightfully silly. A fountain sprayed lemonade-shaped water. Neko grew a fluffy
white moustache. Elsa’s snowflakes turned square and stacked into little castles.

“My moustache makes me look wise,” Neko said.

“It makes you look like a ticklish cloud,” said Magic Girl.

The moustache tickled Neko’s nose. She sneezed, and it flew onto the sandwich
mayor. Everyone laughed, including the mayor.

Elsa showed the children how to tap their wands twice to undo magic they didn’t
want. The shoes stopped quacking. The cabbages voted to keep their bow ties.

When evening rolled in, the snowflake door came back. Elsa waved goodbye and
said she’d never forget Otherworld’s wonderfully weird concert. The children
held up their wands and made a sparkly path for her.

Neko purred warmly. “Come back anytime. Next week our mayor may be a muffin.”

Elsa stepped through the door laughing while square snowflakes danced behind her
like tiny glowing kites.
