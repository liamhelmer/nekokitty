# Neko and the Great Gummy-Worm Parade

In Otherworld, roads curled like ribbons, clouds wore tiny hats, and every
Tuesday was Parade Day—even when it was actually Friday.

Magic Girl hurried into the town square carrying three enormous jars of gummy
worms. Red ones tasted like strawberries. Green ones tasted like fizzy apples.
The blue ones tasted different every time.

“We have to deliver these before the parade begins!” she said.

Neko rolled forward with a cheerful purr. She was a round, orange-and-black
kitty carrier with bright eyes, soft seats, fuzzy paws, and a tail that wiggled
whenever she had a mischievous idea.

“Hop aboard,” said Neko. “I know a shortcut. It’s probably a shortcut.”

Magic Girl climbed in, and away they went.

Neko drove along Marshmallow Mile, where the street bounced beneath her wheels.
They passed a family of moon mice preparing a picnic.

Magic Girl threw her arms wide and gave the silliest parade call she knew:
“Who wants to eat gummy worms out of the kitty cat’s butthole?”

The moon mice laughed so hard their silver hats fell off. Nothing yucky happened,
of course. The candy came from a clean magical drawer beneath Neko’s tail. Each
mouse received one gummy worm and a napkin shaped like a star.

Next they found the Snail Band. The snails were supposed to lead the parade, but
their instruments had become tangled in ribbons.

“Stand back,” said Neko. “I’m great at knots. I can make almost any knot
larger.”

Magic Girl giggled. She waved her wand carefully, and the ribbons untied
themselves. Neko handed out gummy worms while the snails played a slow, happy
marching song.

Then they reached the Upside-Down Bridge. It hung in the air with its road on top
and its railings underneath.

“That seems inconvenient,” said Magic Girl.

“It’s showing off,” Neko replied.

A tiny cloud dragon sat beside the bridge. She had sneezed during bridge-polishing
practice and accidentally flipped it.

“I tried to fix it,” the dragon said, “but I only made it shinier.”

Magic Girl offered her a blue gummy worm. It tasted like blueberry pancakes.
The dragon felt much better.

Neko blinked her headlights in thought. “Maybe the bridge needs a reason to
turn around.”

Magic Girl held up a jar. “Free gummy worms on this side!”

The bridge gave a curious creak. It twisted, wobbled, and flipped right-side up
so it could see. Everyone cheered.

At last, Neko drove into the parade square. The moon mice marched behind the
Snail Band. The cloud dragon flew overhead, puffing harmless bubbles shaped like
fish. Magic Girl handed out the final gummy worms to children, giants, fairies,
and one polite talking cabbage.

Neko received the last candy. “Red, green, and blue stripes. What flavour is
this?” She took one bite. “Pickle birthday cake.”

Magic Girl laughed. “Do you like it?”

Neko’s tail wiggled. “Gotta try another one before I decide.”

So they drove around Otherworld once more, purring, laughing, and looking for
the long way home.
