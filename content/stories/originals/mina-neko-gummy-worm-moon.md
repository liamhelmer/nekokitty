# Magic Girl, Neko, and the Gummy-Worm Moon

Magic Girl was a little girl with magic, but her magic never behaved exactly as expected. A flower
might grow a green hat. A silver bell might become a silver sock. Once, when
she tried to sparkle, she sneezed three bubbles and a polite hiccup.

She practiced beside Neko, the orange-and-black kitty carrier with fuzzy paws,
a long tail, and a small rear treat hatch. “That hatch carries important
supplies,” Neko said.

“What supplies?” Magic Girl asked.

“Emergency gummy worms, of course.”

Inside the hatch was a bag of gummy worms. The bag wiggled. One purple worm
poked out, wearing a crumb as a hat. “Otherworld needs help!” it squeaked. “The
moon has gone square!”

Their moon was round. “Different moon,” Neko explained. “Otherworld has its own.
Very fancy. Sometimes inconvenient.” A sticky circle became a glowing door, and
Magic Girl climbed aboard. Neko rolled through with her tail waving behind.

Otherworld was almost normal. Clouds floated upside down. Teacups grew on bushes.
Ducks wore rain boots on their heads. Above it all hung a yellow moon shaped like
a cracker, dropping crumbs onto everyone.

“Needs cheese,” said Neko, as a crumb landed on her roof.

At Wiggly Hill, red, green, blue, and golden gummy worms waited in neat rows.
They bowed to Magic Girl. “We heard Magic Girl was coming.”

“I’m still practicing,” Magic Girl said.

“Perfect,” said Neko. “Practicing people notice things.”

Magic Girl looked again. A rainbow string dangled from one corner of the square moon.
The worms had tried to pull it, but they were too squishy. Magic Girl wrapped it around
her hands. Neko braced her fuzzy paws. The worms made a wiggly line behind them.

“Pull on three!” Magic Girl called. “One, two—banana!”

Everyone pulled early because banana was not three. The moon spun. Crumbs flew.
The ducks cheered and caught them in baskets.

“Again,” Magic Girl laughed. “This time, really on three.” They pulled together. The
moon stretched into a triangle, a star, and then a shape like Neko’s head.

“My most flattering angle,” said Neko.

But it would not become round. Magic Girl remembered that her strange magic always did
something useful, even when it surprised her. “I need one gummy worm of every
color.” Seven volunteers linked into a candy rainbow in her hands.

“Round and bright, soft moonlight, wiggle wrong and wiggle right!” Magic Girl said.
Then she sneezed.

Seven bubbles floated up, each carrying a worm. They bounced against the moon’s
corners: boing, boing, boing! The corners softened, and the moon puffed into a
perfect yellow circle.

Warm moonlight filled Otherworld. Teacup bushes clinked. Upside-down clouds
rained upward. The ducks danced in their head boots.

“You fixed it!” said the purple worm.

“We fixed it,” Magic Girl said.

The worms gave her a medal shaped like a gummy worm. It was not candy, but Neko
licked it once to be sure. Back home, the hatch was full again.

“One for emergencies, one for adventures, and two because I said so,” Neko said.

Magic Girl chose a gummy worm. It waved before she ate it. At least, she was fairly
sure it did.
