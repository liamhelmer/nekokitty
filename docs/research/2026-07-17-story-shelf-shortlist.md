# First open story-shelf shortlist

Retrieved 2026-07-17. These are candidates, not yet downloaded or runtime
enabled. Keep source text, attribution and a change notice separate from any
derived RAG card.

| Work | Why it fits | Rights and source |

| [Clever cat](https://africanstorybook.org/newviewer/index.php?bt=3&dual=false&id=8878) | Short, gentle, absurd house-cat story; ages 5--7 | Ursula Nafula; illustrations Catherine Groenewald; African Storybook Initiative 2014; CC BY 4.0; level “First sentences.” |
| [Counting cats](https://www.africanstorybook.org/read/downloadbooklet.php?id=12156&layout=booklet) | Playful 0--10 participation story | Nina Orange; illustrations Jesse Breytenbach; African Storybook Initiative 2015; CC BY 4.0; “First words.” |

| [Wild Cat! Wild Cat!](https://storyweaver.org.in/en/stories/21618-wild-cat-wild-cat) | Repetitive, playful house-cat/wild-cat comparison | Sejal Mehta; illustrations Rohan Chakravarty; Pratham Books 2017; CC BY 4.0; English Level 1. Tag as light cat nonfiction/call-and-response. |
| [Tiger, Tiger, Where Are You?](https://store.prathambooks.org/productDetails?productId=tiger_tiger_where_are_you) | Hide-and-seek tiger tracking; 2--3 minute narrated version | Mujahid Khan; illustrations Manjari Chakravarti; Pratham Books 2019; CC BY 4.0; English Level 1/2. Review light factual scat/hair clues before enabling. |

African Storybook’s primary page gives full text and download controls for
`Clever cat`; StoryWeaver’s [open-content policy](https://storyweaver.org.in/en/open-content)
states its books/illustrations are CC BY 4.0. Before ingestion, retrieve each
canonical file, verify displayed attribution/licence, record hash and source
date, extract text, add a child-tone review, and build a compact RAG card rather
than exposing raw long text to the language model.

Excluded on tone: a missing-cat distress story and a cat/dog story with threats
of beating/eating.
